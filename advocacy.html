Think Before You Share

Digital Media Literacy Advocacy

In today’s digital world, information spreads faster than ever before. Social media allows people to share news, opinions, and experiences instantly, but not everything we see online is accurate or trustworthy. Fake news and misleading posts can create confusion, cause unnecessary panic, and sometimes lead to serious harm in communities.

Before sharing a post, always ask yourself:

  • Is this from a trusted and reliable source?
  • Is there clear evidence supporting the information?
  • Is the content recent, verified, and accurate?

Being a responsible media user means thinking critically and verifying facts before clicking the share button. One incorrect post can spread misinformation to hundreds or even thousands of people. Let us use technology wisely by promoting truth, respect, and awareness online.
